How the survey upgrades this simulation
The original simulation used generic placeholder assumptions. After analysing 163 real responses from Mladost & Druzhba residents, we've identified 8 high-impact improvements that would make this simulation measurably more realistic for planning, investor presentations, and operational decisions.
SKU Catalogue

1. Add Survey-Validated Products

Current SKUs are generic. Survey reveals top demanded items: fresh salads (×15), ready sandwiches (×5), smoothies/freshes (×7), and local dairy (×11). Add a 5th SKU category: "Fresh & Ready" (salads, sandwiches, smoothies) with shorter expiry windows and dynamic markdown logic. This is the single biggest gap between simulation and real demand.

Traffic Curve

2. Survey-Calibrated Peak Hours

54% of respondents shop several times per week; 66% are employed workers. Real peaks should be 7–9am (commute in) and 17–19pm (commute out), with a secondary lunch peak 12–13pm. Current simulation weights are too flat mid-day. Night transactions (22:00–06:00) should remain non-zero — 57% rated 24/7 availability 4 or 5 out of 5.

Pricing

3. Price Sensitivity Scenario

46% cite high prices as their main pain point — the #1 complaint. The simulation should model two pricing scenarios: "Premium only" vs "Tiered (entry + premium)". A tiered approach could increase transaction volume by an estimated 20–30% among price-sensitive 18–24 year old students and school pupils who represent 25% of respondents.

Location Score

4. Two-Location Comparative Simulation

Survey data clearly points to two distinct top locations: Druzhba 1 lakeside (sports/recreation crowd, 12 mentions) and Mladost 1 metro exit (commuter crowd, 10 mentions). The simulation should support side-by-side comparison of revenue KPIs per location, using different foot traffic profiles (sports/recreation vs transit commuter patterns).

Audience Mix

5. Student-Hour Demand Spike

School pupils and university students make up 18% of respondents and specifically request affordable snacks for "eating on the go". Add a school_hour_boost parameter (13:00–15:00 on weekdays) with +40% transaction volume spike and lower average basket (~8 BGN), triggering the existing upsell mode automatically.

Demand Driver

6. Gluten-Free / Allergen Flag

Gluten-free is the most cross-cutting specific need (13 explicit mentions), followed by lactose-free (4) and no added sugar (7). Add a dietary_flag_demand metric to inventory: SKUs without a gluten-free variant should be flagged. When flag_demand > 10% of transactions, auto-trigger a SKU_GAP alert recommending a new product line.

Risk

7. Price-Driven Churn Scenario

If the simulation's price boost action is used >2× in a week, it should now trigger a PRICE_CHURN risk — modelling that 46% price-sensitive segment would reduce visit frequency. This reflects a real operational risk completely missing from the current version: the tension between margin optimisation and volume retention.

Realism

8. Seasonal & Weekend Modifiers

Druzhba lakeside and park locations show high weekend demand (jogging, cycling, recreation). Add a weekend_multiplier (+35% transactions Sat/Sun for park locations, −15% for Business Park locations which are weekday-dominant). Summer should boost isotonics ×1.5 (per business plan), winter boosts protein bars ×1.3. The seasonal action button now applies this.
